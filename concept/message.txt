Detail 2
1. Player and Weapon Visual Presentation (Display & Visuals)
The core visual concept of your game is the **“Circular Array”**, emphasizing the balance between a microscopic organism aesthetic and industrial machinery feel.
Player Character (The Flower Core)
* Status Feedback: The core (player center) is not merely a static image.
    * Health Display: Core color shifts with health levels. Fully healed, it appears crystal clear; critically low health causes it to dim and crack.
    * Environmental Adaptation: Entering different biomes triggers subtle visual changes. For example, in Arctic biomes, frost forms on the core's surface; in Industrial biomes, flowing digital matrices or circuit textures appear.
* Layered Structure:
    1. Base Layer: Aura, displaying the current biome's Affect effect (e.g., desert fire glow).
    2. Middle Layer: Core (character body).
    3. Top Layer: Petal Array.
Weapon Display: Petals
* Slot Visuals: Petals do not adhere to the Core but float around it.
    * Orbital Effect: Slots unlocked at different tiers can be distributed across varying “orbital heights,” forming inner and outer rings for a more complex, powerful appearance.
    * Rarity Light Effects:
        * Common (White): Simple organic texture.
        * Ultra (Red/Gold): Features particle trail effects with dynamic scaling or deformation animations (e.g., pulsing like breathing).
* Equipment Preview: In the UI, hovering over a petal displays its rotational path curve, visually indicating attack range.

2. Core Combat Mechanics
Rotation & Physics
* Dynamic RPM: Rotation speed isn't a static value.
    * Inertia System: When moving, petals slightly drift in the opposite direction due to inertia (drag effect), adding tactile feedback to controls.
    * Collision Decay: When petals strike heavy enemies, RPM temporarily dips slightly to simulate “resistance.”
* Attack Mechanics:
    * Sweet Spot: The outer edge of petals deals maximum damage. Damage decreases if enemies are too close to the core, encouraging players to maintain distance.
    * Knockback: Different petals possess distinct physical masses. For example, Ocean's bubble petals deal low damage but can knock enemies extremely far.
Phase Split - Special Mechanic
* Activation: Press Control or a specific key.
* Visual Effect: Player core and petals instantly blur, become semi-transparent, and slightly expand outward.
* Mechanic Details: (Example)
    * During split, the player is unselectable and immune to all collisions and bullets.
    * Cost: Cannot attack while split.
    * Industrial Zone Bonus: If damage is avoided during the split initiation in Industrial maps, triggers a “Perfect Dodge,” significantly boosting the critical hit rate of the next attack for a short duration.

3. Progression System Details (RPG Progression)
The Forge (Synthesis & Evolution)
* Recipe Mechanics: More than just 5+1.
    * Material Influence: Adding “Essence” from specific biomes (e.g., Swamp slime) during synthesis imbues petals with poison properties.
    * Ultra Awakening: When synthesizing Ultra petals, they randomly gain an “Entry” (e.g., +10% Attack Range or Restores 1%$ HP per Kill).
4. Industrial Zone and Story Mode Details (The Industrial Logic)
* The Narrative:
    * Environmental Storytelling: Players will encounter massive, decommissioned “First Generation Flower” prototypes throughout the Industrial Zone.
    * Gear Collection: Gathering 8 gears isn't just for unlocking doors—each collected gear adds a mechanical beat to the background music (BGM), culminating in a full industrial heavy metal track during the final boss battle.
* Evidence System:
    * Players will discover wreckage of the “False Sky” projector at the map's edge, proving all biomes exist within a massive factory.

Player Visuals & Mechanics
UI Display
* Mana Bar: After unlocking the secondary talent following Health, a blue energy bar appears below your health bar.
* Talent Tree Preview: The talent interface doesn't display everything directly; instead, it shows “shadows.” When your Damage reaches a certain threshold, the Poison and Fire icons will illuminate, indicating they are selectable.
Game Mechanics
* Elemental Synergy:
    * Combining Lightning (lightning damage) with Freeze (freezing skill) triggers a “Superconductivity” effect, dealing double lightning damage to frozen enemies.
    * Poison can be paired with Reach to kite opponents from a distance with your toxic petals, continuously stacking layers.
Practice Details
* When practicing skills, a progress light effect appears at the screen's center.
* For Split skills, you'll see the core practicing how to “split itself in two.”

Lv upgrade
Lv 11: RPG World Unlocked: Exit the Arena and enter Grassland. Gain first off-hand slot.
Lv 15: Petal Synthesis Furnace (Craft): Unlock synthesis system. Evolve 5 base petals into “Rare” quality.
Lv 20: Attribute Talent Tree (Talents): Unlocks the stat allocation system: Can boost [Armor], [RPM], or [Critical Hit].
Lv 25: Achievement (Achievement): Unlocks achievements, granting generous rewards.
Lv 30: Secondary Slot (Secondary): Grants a secondary slot to equip auxiliary petals.
Lv 42: Affect Tweak: Allows players to slightly mitigate environmental debuffs (e.g., Arctic freeze slow).
Lv 50: Carapace: Unlocks a torso equipment slot. Shells dropped by defeated bosses provide passive shields.
Lv 55: Awaken: Unlocks an active ultimate ability (CD 60s) based on your most equipped petal type.
Lv 64: Double Boost: An exceptionally powerful ability: retains 20% of the previous biome's buffs while in the current biome.
Lv 70: Upgrade Slot: The final petal slot, typically used for the “Core Petal.”
Lv 75: Ascension: Qualifies you to challenge the final Factory Boss and unlocks full-map teleportation.

Biome
Biome Effect
1. Garden —— 【Gravity Potential】
Effect: Simulates the physics of vertical space.
Impact: Movement speed increases by $+25\%$ when moving downward on-screen, and Body Damage increases by $20\%$. Movement speed decreases by $15\%$ when moving upward.
Core Logic: Encourages players to soar like eagles to high ground, then dive down for devastating strikes.
2. Desert — 【Heat Overload】
Effect: Extreme heat reduces defence but heightens aggression.
Effect: Player Armour $-50\%$, but all attacks deal 10% additional Fire splash damage and slightly increased Reach.
Core Logic: Glass cannon mode. You become fragile, but each attack ignites enemies.
3. Ocean — 【Hydraulic Boost】
Effect: Water resistance and bubble interaction.
Impact: Base movement speed $-20\%$, but all bubble-type petals increase in volume by $+50\%$ and knockback doubles.
Core Logic: Alter combat pacing by controlling battlefield distance with large bubbles, preventing enemy close-quarters engagement.
4. Jungle — 【Hyper-Photosynthesis】
Effect: Extremely high oxygen levels accelerate cellular repair.
Impact: HP Region $+100\%$, but viscous air reduces petal rotation speed (Reload) by $-25\%$.
Core Logic: Stationary defence play style. You recover health extremely quickly, allowing greater margin for error, but your attack frequency slows.
5. Savanna — [Tailwind Flow]
Effect: Strong seasonal winds alter movement dynamics.
Effect: Movement speed $+50\%$ when moving with the wind. When attacking with the wind, petal projectile range (Reach) increases by 100%.
Core Logic: Ranged kiting. Players must constantly monitor wind direction indicators to execute “kiting” tactics.
6. Swamp — [Corrosive Methane]
Effect: Toxic gas erodes armour while enhancing weapon penetration.
Effect: Global damage $+30\%$, but player's maximum health (Max HP) temporarily reduced by 20%.
Core Logic: Rapid elimination. Swiftly eliminate enemies before maximum health is reduced, ideal for high burst damage builds.
7. Sewer — 【Bio-Mutation】
Effect: Contaminants stimulate neural responses.
Effect: Critical Chance $+40\%$, but damage taken increases by $20\%$.
Core Logic: Gambler Mode. Utilize extremely high critical probability to instantly clear enemies, but mistakes come at a heavy cost.
8. Factory — 【Static Overclock】
Effect: Electromagnetic radiation from mechanical facilities enhances petal efficiency.
Effect: Reload Speed $+50\%$, but all skill cooldowns (CD) extend by 50%.
Core Logic: Pure flat A-flow. Sacrifices skill damage to rely on rapidly spinning petals forming a “meat grinder”-like defensive circle.
9. Marine — 【Deep Pressure】
Effect: High-pressure environments compress biological density.
Result: Defence (Armour) $+60\%$, but Movement Speed $-40\%$.
Core Logic: Steel tank. Extremely slow movement, but enemies struggle to penetrate its formidable armor.
10. Arctic — 【Superconductive】
Effect: Low temperatures eliminate electrical resistance, enhancing neural conduction.
Impact: Skill cooldown (CD) $-40\%$, but collision damage increased by $25\%$.
Core Logic: Skill-focused.
